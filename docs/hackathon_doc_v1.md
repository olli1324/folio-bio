# AI Agent Olympics — Milan AI Week Hackathon

_Reference summary · compiled 15 May 2026_

Sources: the lablab.ai hackathon page, the official Luma event listing, the
Featherless setup guide, and the verbatim sponsor-challenge text our team
collected in Discord. Where sources disagree, the official Luma listing is
treated as authoritative and the conflict is noted.

## What it is

The AI Agent Olympics is the official builder event of Milan AI Week 2026, run
by lablab.ai. The brief is autonomous AI agents — systems that reason, plan,
and carry out real-world tasks, rather than chat interfaces. Teams build during
an online phase, then converge in Milan for an in-person finale where selected
teams present on stage in front of the Milan AI Week audience.

lablab.ai runs it; AI WEEK is the host conference. The event is also flagged as
featured by Google DeepMind on the listing.

## When and where

The competition window runs roughly 13–20 May 2026. The online build phase is
already underway (our team has been building since 13 May). The in-person
finale is at **Fiera Milano, Rho, Milan** across two days:

- **Tuesday 19 May** — build and submission day. Submission deadline is
  **17:00**.
- **Wednesday 20 May** — demos, live pitches, and the winners ceremony.

Travel and accommodation are not covered. A free Milan AI Week conference
ticket is included for participants.

## Build themes

The organisers frame five areas teams can build across:

- **Intelligent Reasoning** — agents that make independent decisions and adapt
  when conditions change.
- **Agentic Workflows** — systems that plan, call tools and APIs, and manage
  multi-step tasks.
- **Enterprise Utility** — solving real problems for the founders and operators
  at AI Week.
- **Multimodal Intelligence** — agents working with documents, images, audio,
  or video.
- **Collaborative Systems** — multi-agent setups working together on complex
  goals.

These are themes, not the prize tracks. The prize tracks are the sponsor
challenges below.

## Sponsor challenges

The official partners listed are Vultr, Google, Kraken, and Featherless AI.
Speechmatics also runs a challenge (confirmed from the organiser text our team
received). Each partner runs its own challenge with its own prizes; a project
is submitted under a challenge tag. The organisers confirmed in Discord that
one team can build a single application that addresses multiple sponsor
challenges — splitting into separate *submissions* would mean separate teams.

- **Featherless** — build a domain-specialised, open-source AI agent using
  models from the Featherless catalogue. They want a narrow, single-domain
  agent rather than a generalist; an async-first architecture (background jobs,
  scheduled workflows, document pipelines); a fully open-source release under a
  permissive licence with reproducible setup; and something production-shaped.
  _This is Oliver's piece._
- **Speechmatics** — conversational AI, voice agents, or real-time systems
  built on Speechmatics speech-to-text. They highlight real-time transcription
  inside agent workflows, speaker diarization, and batch transcription.
- **Vultr** — a web-based enterprise agent deployed on Vultr, built for
  real-world workflows across operations, sales, marketing, support, and HR.
  They want multi-step agentic workflows and a production-style web app.
- **Kraken** — a trading-focused challenge. Public information indicates top
  agents get a final audit from Kraken and winners are decided on net profit
  and loss over the competition window. A "one submission per participant or
  team" rule was mentioned specifically in connection with the Kraken
  challenge — worth confirming whether it applies more broadly.
- **Google** — listed as a partner with a link to the Gemini API model docs,
  so a Gemini-based challenge is likely. Exact challenge text was not in the
  sources reviewed; confirm on the platform if relevant.

## Prizes

The official listing states a prize pool of **$28,000+** spread across
categories including infrastructure, AI models, and real-world applications
(one secondary source cited $32,000+). Featherless also provides a free month
of Feather Premium for the hackathon, and Vultr has offered participant
credits.

## Judging criteria

For this hackathon, projects are judged on how well the chosen models are
integrated into the solution, how clear and effective the presentation is, the
real-world impact and practical value for business use cases, and the
uniqueness and creativity of the approach. General lablab judging also weighs
technical execution and functionality. The presentation weight is worth noting
— the live pitch on 20 May counts, not just the build.

## Submission requirements

Teams submit the agent code, documentation, and a demo (video or live).
Projects should be web-based AI agents for real-world enterprise workflows,
showing genuine multi-step agentic behaviour in a production-style application.
The correct sponsor-challenge tag has to be selected at submission so the
project is judged in the right category.

## What this means for our team

BioLitAgent fits the brief cleanly: it is an enterprise-utility agent with a
real multi-step workflow (search, retrieve, extract, synthesise, report). The
plan to build one application that satisfies the Featherless, Speechmatics, and
Vultr challenges is sanctioned by the organisers. Two things to act on: the
presentation carries real judging weight, so the 20 May pitch needs rehearsal
time budgeted; and the submission deadline is firm at 17:00 on 19 May, so the
integration buffer in the build plan should not be spent.

Open items worth confirming on the platform or in Discord: whether the "one
submission per team" rule extends beyond the Kraken challenge, and the exact
Google challenge brief if the team wants to reach for a fourth track.
